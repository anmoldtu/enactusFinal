About Enactus DTU

WE, at Enactus DTU, apply entrepreneurial concepts to develop dynamic projects that take advantage of people’s inherent dexterity and aptitude at the center of improving their livelihoods.

Founded in 2011, we began with Project Chhaap where we involved a group of women in production of printed bags and sweatshirts using screen printing. From there, Enactus DTU has evolved in leaps and bounds and currently runs two successful projects in the NCR region, Project Avataran and Project Unnati. What started out as a team of 20 has expanded into a family of 60. Enactus DTU envisions a world where poverty and hunger are unheard of, where gender is not the basis of bias and where environmental sustainability is the foremost concern. At Enactus, we rise by lifting others.

An insight into our ‘3 Phase Framework”

Phase 1: Brainstorming

Involves coming up with an idea, its sustainability, and business model ,and forming a dedicated team for the handling of the project. Days of constructive discussion over each and every idea ensures that only the optimum idea gets through the next phase.

Phase 2: Groundwork

Comprises of finding suitable slum(s) via extensive slum visits and the subsequent analysis. Also, includes our quest to find a suitable entrepreneur, and his training. Slums are selected owing upon their success on various parameters including 1) Need Analysis 2) Availability of the entrepreneur 3) Competition (If Any), and so on.

Phase 3:Execution:

The real test for our previous steps. Encompasses opening of service /manufacturing outlets, and thereafter, the regular publicity and promotions of the outlet/ product. Includes orderly checks and meet-ups with our entrepreneur, corroborating the alignment of our goals with the current scenario.
